Early Life and Education

Alan Dershowitz was born in Brooklyn, New York, and grew up in a Jewish family. His early life set the stage for his later success. He attended Yeshiva University High School, showing early signs of his debating prowess and intellectual capabilities. Dershowitz then went on to graduate from Brooklyn College before earning his law degree from Yale Law School, where he was first in his class and editor of the Yale Law Journal.

Legal Career and Academic Tenure

After clerking for Judge David Bazelon and Justice Arthur Goldberg of the U.S. Supreme Court, Dershowitz embarked on a career that would see him become one of the most well-known attorneys in America. He joined the faculty of Harvard Law School at the age of 25, becoming the youngest full professor in the school’s history. His tenure at Harvard lasted until his retirement in 2013, and it significantly contributed to his net worth through salary and other academic-related income.
